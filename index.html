Carbohydrates Deep Dive

Biological Breakdown: Carbohydrates are molecules consisting of carbon, hydrogen, and oxygen atoms. They are classified into Simple Carbs (Monosaccharides like glucose/fructose) and Complex Carbs (Polysaccharides like starch and fiber).


The Impact: Simple carbs provide an immediate "spike" of energy, whereas complex carbs like brown rice take longer to digest, providing a steady stream of fuel for your brain and muscles.

⚠️ Metabolic Risk: Chronic excess of simple sugars can lead to Insulin Resistance (Diabetes), while a severe deficiency leads to Ketosis and mental fatigue (brain fog).

The Science of Proteins

Structural Levels: Proteins are made of amino acid chains connected by peptide bonds. They exist in four levels: Primary (linear sequence), Secondary (coils/folds), Tertiary (3D shape), and Quaternary (multiple clusters).


Why they matter: They aren't just for muscles. Proteins act as Enzymes (catalysts for life), Hormones (messengers), and Antibodies (defense). Sources include fish, eggs, and plant-based options like mung beans and tofu.

⚠️ Health Note: Over-consumption can strain the kidneys as they filter out nitrogen by-products. Deficiency leads to Muscle Atrophy and a weakened immune system.
